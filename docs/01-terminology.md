# Terminology

This document defines baseline terms used by the TOR specification.

The definitions here are intentionally simple and implementation-neutral.
They provide a common vocabulary for discussion, refinement, and future extension.

---

## Terms of Reference (TOR)

A document that defines the purpose, scope, deliverables, conditions, and expectations of a project, service, or procurement.

In this repository, a TOR is treated as a structured specification artifact rather than only a narrative document.

---

## Objective

A stated goal that the project is intended to achieve.

Objectives describe what the project is trying to accomplish, but do not by themselves define how the work must be carried out.

---

## Requirement

A clearly stated condition, capability, or expectation that must be satisfied.

Requirements should be specific enough to guide implementation or evaluation.

---

## Deliverable

A defined output, artifact, service, or result that must be produced as part of the project.

Deliverables should be assessable in practice.

---

## Scope of Work

The set of activities, tasks, or areas of responsibility included in the project.

Scope clarifies what is in scope and, where useful, what is outside scope.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

Conditions that must be satisfied for a deliverable, service, or result to be accepted.

Acceptance criteria should be reviewable and not depend only on informal judgment.

---

## Constraint

A known limitation or condition that affects how the project can be designed, executed, or delivered.

Examples may include budget, timeline, legal obligations, infrastructure limitations, or dependency on existing systems.
